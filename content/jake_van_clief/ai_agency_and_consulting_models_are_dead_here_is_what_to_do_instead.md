# AI Agency and Consulting Models are Dead — What to Do Instead

Source: <https://youtu.be/AjzBaEkWkNA>

---

## The Old Consulting Model (Being Disrupted)

- **Strategy phase**: Firm (McKenzie, KPMG, Big Four) produces large reports (e.g., 87-page report for $400K) with market research and expert recommendations
- **Implementation phase**: Separate firm handles software, governance, or learning implementation
- **Adoption phase**: Post-build rollout and usage
- This **waterfall model** is structurally broken by AI — not because AI is hype, but because of how rapidly individuals can now self-solve

---

## Why AI Breaks the Traditional Model

- **Subject matter experts can now self-build**: A non-technical employee can prototype a solution in an afternoon with prompts that would have cost $20M and 2 years previously
- **Misallocation problem**: Companies are deploying AI to solve problems that a simple SQL call or Python script would handle better
- **Ignored synergy**: AI paired with traditional software is underutilized; agencies oversell "100% AI" when the right ratio is closer to:
    - ~60% traditional software
    - ~20% rule-based logic / business process design
    - ~10–20% AI implementation
- **Cheap tools are powerful**: A $0–$20/month tool (e.g., ChatGPT) already captures significant value for most use cases

---

## The New Consulting Model

- **Frame**: Treat the engagement like a startup discovery process, not a waterfall delivery
- **Step 1 — Discovery by doing**: Give domain experts a cheap AI tool, have them attempt to solve their own problems with it
    - Result is imperfect (60–80% there), but surfaces real pain points directly
    - Cuts out the middleman between problem and solution
- **Step 2 — Data collection**: Over weeks or months, gather what employees built, what failed, what they were trying to do
- **Step 3 — Expert synthesis**: AI/technical experts review outputs and identify scalable integration paths
- **Step 4 — Package and invest**: Only at this stage define what to build and where to invest budget

---

## Key Strategic Claims

- **Individuation as the new value-add**: The highest-value consulting output is now bespoke, personalized experiences — e.g., individual student assessments, custom insurance walkthroughs — not generic strategy reports
- **Scope expansion is inevitable**: As technology makes individual tasks easy, consultants must cover more adjacent territory to remain valuable (analogous to accountants needing to know Excel, tax law, and bank transactions)
- **Value must exceed self-service threshold**: If a client can do it themselves (or a $20/month subscription does it), that cannot be your product — it must become a feature
- **Copilot Studio threat**: A Microsoft Copilot subscription is positioned to replace the majority of current n8n/workflow automation vendors and AI agencies; most clients don't know this yet
- **AI bubble warning**: An AI crash is expected (analogous to dot-com bust); firms providing real value will extract value from the crash; firms riding hype will not survive

---

## Actionable Practices

- **Upskill employees first** before building anything — have them interact with AI tools on real problems
- **Audit for over-engineering**: Check whether current AI initiatives could be solved with traditional software
- **Run small pilots** with domain experts using consumer-grade AI tools to generate discovery data
- **Build agile, small teams**: A 10–20 person AI-augmented team can outcompete a 40–50 person traditional team if governance and process are in place
- **Reframe your value proposition**: Ask — what is the layer above what clients can now do for free?
