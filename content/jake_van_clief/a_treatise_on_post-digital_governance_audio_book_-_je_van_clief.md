# A Treatise on Post-Digital Governance

Source: <https://youtu.be/OlBFN3Z9S4A>

## Core Framework: Post-Digital Governance

---

- **Post-digital**: paradigm where digital technologies are so normalized they are taken as given — like electricity or print; not "beyond digital" but digital as foundational infrastructure
- **Post-digital governance**: evolution of administrative structures in an age where digital is omnipresent; focus shifts from *adoption* of tools to understanding their societal, ethical, and behavioral implications
- Five pillars of post-digital governance challenges:
  1. **Ethics** — data privacy, surveillance, algorithmic rights
  2. **Digital inclusivity** — preventing digital divides from worsening inequality
  3. **Reinvention of structures** — pre-digital governance models become inadequate
  4. **Human-digital symbiosis** — blurring of physical/digital boundaries
  5. **Cultural shifts** — digital natives reshaping governance expectations

---

## Dimensional Model of Commerce and Value

---

- Author's original framework: each major transport/communication technology adds a new *dimension* of value movement
    - **3rd dimension**: physical movement of goods (horse carts, foot)
    - **4th dimension (railroads/shipping)**: value in *movement itself*, not just goods — Vanderbilts, East India Co.; vertical/horizontal integration
    - **5th dimension (internet)**: virtual movement — e-commerce, digital storefronts, direct-to-consumer; website + product = combined value unit
    - **5th dimension extension (blockchain)**: decentralized, secure movement of *both* assets *and* the currency used to purchase them — eliminates financial intermediary
- Pattern across each transition: **elimination of the middleman** at each dimensional shift
- PayPal/Zelle identified as transitional first steps into the 5th dimension, still requiring institutional verification

## Social Media and the Dual Economy

---

- **Prosumer economy**: workers are simultaneously producers and consumers of digital content
- Key mechanism: physical labor generates *two* revenue streams — (1) direct product/service value, (2) digital documentation/audience value (ad revenue, sponsorships)
    - Example: pressure washers giving services *free*, recouping costs via TikTok ad revenue (up to $20k/month at 1M followers)
- YouTube Partner Program paid out $30B+ to creators by 2022
- **Platform capitalism** (term from Nick Srnicek's book *Platform Capitalism*): five platform types identified:
  1. Advertising (Google, Facebook)
  2. Cloud (Amazon Web Services)
  3. Industrial (GE)
  4. Product (Spotify)
  5. Lean (Uber, Airbnb)
- Facebook ad revenue: $84B (2020); AWS net sales: $45B (2020)
- **WeChat case**: 34% of China's total data traffic; ~1/3 of China's GDP attributed to it (2016); Russia's Sberbank accepted WeChat Pay — model for circumventing Western financial systems
- Paradox: capitalist monetization infrastructure used to achieve communal/redistributive ends — synthesis of Smith and Marx

## Blockchain: Technical and Economic Analysis

---

- **Byzantine Generals Problem**: foundational challenge for decentralized consensus — how do distributed nodes agree without a trusted central authority when some nodes may be malicious
- **Proof of Work**: consensus mechanism where miners compete to solve computational problems; winner adds block, earns cryptocurrency; makes fraudulent chain manipulation economically infeasible
- **Hash**: cryptographic output linking blocks; required for network access and integrity
- Blockchain as **distributed ledger**: copies across all nodes; no single point of control; retroactive alteration requires simultaneous consensus of majority of network
- Key property: **immutability** — transaction history cannot be altered without network-wide agreement
- Applications flagged: cryptocurrencies, supply chain management, voting systems, transparent political finance tracking
- Blockchain and virtual nations: constitutions, economic systems, governance mechanisms could be encoded in tamper-proof digital ledgers
- Tension noted: blockchain promises decentralization but current dominant platforms (Facebook, Twitter) represent re-centralization

## Virtual Nations

---

- **Virtual Nations**: communities unified by shared digital experiences, values, and ideologies rather than geography or ethnicity
- Enabled by: social media, blockchain governance, shared ideological ecosystems
- Citizenship redefined: based on digital participation, network contribution, or alignment with community ethos
- Could issue own cryptocurrencies, operate independent of global financial institutions
- Real-world analog: neighbors with opposing ideologies (Trump vs. Sanders supporters) who inhabit entirely separate information ecosystems despite physical proximity
- Governance implication: political legitimacy increasingly decoupled from territorial sovereignty

## Cyber Warfare and Digital Power

---

- **2015 Ukraine power grid attack**: Sandworm (Russian state-linked) used BlackEnergy malware to disable power for 200,000+ people; preceded physical military invasion — demonstration of cyber as warfare prerequisite
- **Stuxnet (2010)**: targeted Iranian nuclear enrichment facility; redefined acts of war in digital context
- Shift: **soft power → hard power** through cyber means; small actors (even individuals) can match state-level damage
- **DDOS attacks**: North Korea used rudimentary consumer-grade equipment effectively (per Boo Hyong Wook, 2017)
- Dark web enables sanction evasion (North Korea, Russia): drugs, weapons, forged documents transacted with anonymity
- Morgenthau's power framework applied: digital manipulation, misinformation, and narrative control are exertions of power in the digital domain
- Non-state actors and individuals now viable geopolitical actors via cyber capability

## AI: Economic and Political Implications

---

- PwC (2020): AI contributions to global economy projected to exceed **$15.7 trillion by 2030**
- Marx's **organic composition of capital** (Capital Vol. 1): constant capital (machinery/tech) substituted for variable capital (labor) to cut wages → AI as apotheosis of this dynamic
- **Law of Increasing Poverty** (Marx): technological displacement deepens wealth inequality — AI resurrects this concern at unprecedented scale
- Historical AI milestones referenced:
    - AlphaGo defeated Lee Sedol (2016); AlphaZero self-taught chess/shogi/go to superhuman level in hours
    - IBM Watson: medical decision support via massive database analysis
- **Conscious AI governance problem**: author argues governance need not resolve *whether* AI is conscious — only that when a significant portion of the public *believes* it is, democratic governance must respond
    - Precedent: India banned dolphin captivity after Declaration of Rights for Cetaceans recognized dolphins as "non-human persons"
    - Prediction: eco-terrorist-style AI rights factions will emerge in post-digital era
- Mary Shelley's *Frankenstein* invoked as governance cautionary tale: denial of rights/acceptance to a conscious created being leads to systemic ruin

## Regulatory Landscape

---

- **GDPR** (EU): first major framework for personal data collection/processing rights
- **CCPA** (California): right to know, delete, and opt out of sale of personal data
- **India IT Rules 2021**: compliance roles for large social media platforms; rapid removal of harmful content
- **Australia Media Bargaining Law**: compels tech platforms to compensate news publishers
- Tension: data-as-commodity conflicts with privacy regulation; information freedom intertwined with the right to *sell* it
